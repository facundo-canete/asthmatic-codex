# A

## Accession

### Definitions:

1. The time when someone starts a position of authority, especially a king or queen; e.g., '1926 was the year of Emperor Hirohito's accession to the throne';
2. The ntime when a country officially joins a group of countries or signs an agreement; e.g., 'Poland's accession to the EU';
3. An addition to a collection, such as a book or a work of art; e.g., 'There are several new accessions to the library'.

### Translation:
Asunción.


## Acquaintance

### Definitions:

1. A person that you know but who is not a close friend; e.g., 'Claire has a wide circle of friends and acquaintances'.
2. Slight friendship; e.g., 'He hoped their acquaintance would develop further', 'A man *with* whom I had a passing acquaintance'.
3. Knowledge of something; e.g., 'I had little acquaintance *with* modern poetry'.

### Translations:
Conocido, da; conocimiento de algo.


## Allegedly

### Definition:
Used when something is said to be true but has not been proved, like something illegal or wrong; e.g., 'That's where he allegedly killed his wife', 'She was arrested for allegedly stealing a car', 'It's a collection of recipes which allegedly can be prepared in 30 minutes', 'Two oil companies allegedly spent $6 million trying to free two surviving grey whales trapped in the Arctic ice'.

### Translation:
Supuestamente.


## Amiable

### Definition:
Pleasant, friendly, likeable and good-tempered; e.g., 'He seemed an amiable young man', 'So amiable was the mood of the meeting that a decision was soon reached', 'Sally has an amiable manner'.

### Translation:
Amable.


## Assertion

### Definition:
A statement saying that you strongly believe something to be true; e.g., 'He was correct in his assertion that the minister had been lying', 'Do you have any evidence to support your assertions?'.

### Translation:
Afirmación.


## Autological

### Definition:
An autological word expresses a property that it also possesses. A word which is not autological is heterological, except for the word 'heterological' itself; q.v. Grelling-Nelson paradox.

## Dim

### Definitions:

1. (about light) Not bright; e.g., the dim glow of the fire in the grate.
2. (about a place) Where you cannot see well because there is not much light; e.g., a dim room/street.
3. (about a shape) That you cannot see well because there is not much light; e.g., the dim outline of a house in the moonlight.
4. (about eyes) Not able to see well; e.g., 'His eyesight is getting dim'.
5. (about memories) That you cannot remember or imagine clearly; e.g., dim memories, in the dim and distant past, 'She had a dim recollection of the visit'.
6. (about a person) Not intelligent; e.g., 'He's very dim'.
7. (about a situation) Not giving any reason to have hope; i.e., not good; e.g., 'Her future career prospects look dim'.

### Translations:
